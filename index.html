Mini Chocolate Cake




The Mini Chocolate Cake Recipe is inspired the Cake Merchant's "Teeny Tiny Chocolate Cake for One". I really enjoyed this recipe, and so I decided to share it with you guys! It is such a simple and delicate cake that has a rich and pure tase. This cake is perfect for a tiny and quick snack when you don't want to have any leftovers. I hope you enjoy this recipe as much as I did! ~Aishani




Ingredients:

    1/4 cup (60 ml) milk

    1/4 teaspoon apple cider vinegar

    1 1/2 tablespoons canola or vegetable oil

    2 1/2 tablespoons granulated sugar

    1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

    1/4 cup (31 grams) all-purpose flour

    1 1/2 tablespoons cocoa powder

    1/8 teaspoon baking soda

    1/8 teaspoon baking powder



    For the Buttercream Frosting:

    2 tablespoons (28 grams) unsalted butter (or vegan butter), at room temperature and cut into small pieces

    1/3 (35 grams) cup powdered sugar

    1 teaspoon cocoa powder

    1-2 teaspoons milk




Instructions:

    1)Preheat the oven to 350 degrees fahrenheit.

    2) Grease and line a 4-inch cake pan with parchment paper.

    3)In a measuring cup or small bowl, whisk together the milk and vinegar and set aside for a couple of minutes.

    4)Add the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt and whisk until just combined. Careful not to over mix or the cake will become dense.

    5)Pour the batter into the cake pan and bake for about 18 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. You may need to add a couple of minutes to the baking time if you use cake strips

    6)Cool on a wire rack for 15 minutes.

    7) Turn the cake out of the pan and cool completely before frosting.



    For The ButterCream Frosting:

    1)In a small bowl, use a wooden spoon to cream the butter until it becomes smooth.

    2)Once the butter is smooth, add half the powdered sugar and store slowly until it is incorporated into the butter.

    3)Add the rest of the powdered sugar a little bit at a time until the mixture has thickened.

    4)Mix in the cocoa powder

    5)Add the milk and use a whisk the frosting until it becomes light and fluffy.

    Spread the frosting on top of the cooled cake and top with sprinkles.
